**Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR):** Approximately **9.8% per year** (including dividends) since the index's inception in 1926[^1]

**After inflation:** Approximately **6% per year**[^1]

## Key Statistics Since 1926:

- **Time Period:** 1926 to present (nearly 100 years)[^1]
- **Volatility:** Standard deviation of 20.81% on a monthly basis[^1]
- **Positive Years:** The index has posted annual increases approximately **70% of the time**[^1]
- **Negative Years:** Despite declines exceeding 30% in several years, the long-term trend has been strongly positive[^1]

## Index History:

The S&P index was originally composed of 90 companies when first created in 1926 (called the "Composite Index" or "S&P 90")[^2]. It expanded to include 500 components in 1957, which is the composition we know today[^2].

## Important Note:

The returns cited above include both price appreciation and reinvested dividends, which is the most accurate representation of total shareholder returns[^2].

This ~10% annualized return demonstrates that despite numerous market crashes, recessions, and economic downturns over the past century, the S&P 500 has consistently delivered strong long-term growth for investors.
